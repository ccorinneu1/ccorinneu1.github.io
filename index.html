About Me

Hi! I'm Christina Unkenholz, a senior at Cornell University majoring in Computer Science with a minor in Information Science. I'm passionate about using technology to bring ideas to life, solve meaningful problems, and create connections that matter. For me, it’s not just about writing code—it’s about the people behind it and the impact it can have on communities.

Building and fostering communities is a big part of who I am. Whether it’s organizing events that bring people together, creating spaces for mentorship and support, or collaborating on shared goals, I believe that strong communities inspire growth and innovation. This passion for connection drives not just my leadership roles but also how I approach my work: with empathy, teamwork, and a desire to make a difference.

I also value curiosity and balance in life—whether I’m hiking through nature, exploring new places, or discovering a new recipe on my cooking journey. I strive to bring that same sense of exploration and openness to my career, where I aim to grow as a developer while contributing to projects that matter. My goal is simple: to create technology and connections that leave a lasting, positive impact.
