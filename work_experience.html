Business Analyst Intern

Project Overview

During my second internship at Capital One, I focused on optimizing marketing strategies for Capital One Travel by developing a predictive model to determine the best timing for engaging customers. This project addressed a key challenge in the travel sector: understanding when customers are most likely to book travel, enabling the company to align marketing campaigns with customer behavior and maximize engagement.

The predictive model was built using SQL and Python, leveraging large-scale datasets that included customer booking histories, travel preferences, and seasonal trends. I implemented feature engineering techniques to extract relevant insights, such as identifying patterns in travel behaviors and correlating these with external factors like holidays or market conditions. The model incorporated advanced statistical methods to account for variability in customer behavior and predict the optimal points for marketing outreach. These predictions were designed to inform Capital One’s marketing campaigns, ensuring timely and personalized customer interactions.

To support the model, I analyzed the effectiveness of previous campaigns, identifying areas for improvement in timing and targeting. This included using Python for data visualization to highlight key trends and presenting actionable recommendations to senior leadership. The project not only enhanced customer engagement but also aligned with Capital One Travel’s broader revenue strategies by driving more conversions and improving customer satisfaction.

Looking ahead, this work provided a scalable framework for future marketing optimization efforts and demonstrated the potential of predictive analytics to refine customer outreach. The success of this project underscored the importance of data-driven decision-making in creating impactful marketing strategies.

Collaboration Details

During my second internship at Capital One, I worked closely with two other interns on a project aimed at expanding marketing strategies for Capital One Travel to a new subset of customers. While I focused on developing a predictive model to determine the optimal timing for customer engagement, my teammates worked on complementary aspects: one analyzed the persona of the target customers to refine the audience segmentation, and the other explored the channels and content best suited for effective marketing outreach.

As a team, we regularly collaborated to align our individual contributions into a cohesive strategy. I shared insights from my predictive model to validate and enhance the personas and marketing channels identified by my teammates. Additionally, I assisted with SQL queries and data preprocessing to ensure consistency in the datasets used across all components of the project. I helped facilitate team meetings to discuss progress, integrate findings, and align our strategies to deliver maximum impact. Together, we prepared and presented a comprehensive strategy to senior leadership, demonstrating how the model, personas, and channel recommendations could synergize to enhance Capital One Travel’s marketing efficiency and customer engagement. This collaborative effort highlighted the value of integrating technical and strategic approaches to solve complex business challenges.

Project Overview

Using SQL and Python, I analyzed extensive datasets containing loan performance and customer behavior metrics, focusing on the 61% of loans that are exception-priced. This work enabled the creation of a tailored pricing framework designed to improve flexibility and competitiveness while maintaining profitability.
