CS 4320 Teaching Assisstant

As a Teaching Assistant for Cornell University's "Introduction to Database Systems" course, I supported students in mastering key database concepts and skills through lectures, assignments, and hands-on projects. The course covered a wide range of topics, including:

  • Database Fundamentals: Creating and updating databases, entity-relationship diagrams, and schema design.
  • SQL Mastery: Guiding students through simple and advanced SQL queries, including optimization and tuning techniques.
  • Storage and Indexing: Explaining data storage layouts, tree and hash indexes, and buffer management.
  • Query Processing: Teaching relational operators, cost estimation, and advanced query optimization techniques.
  • Concurrency and Recovery: Covering ACID properties, transaction management, isolation, deadlocks, and crash recovery methods.
  • Modern Databases: Introducing distributed systems, NoSQL and NewSQL databases, graph databases, and spatial data processing.

My responsibilities included:

  • Providing personalized assistance during labs and office hours, helping students troubleshoot issues and deepen their understanding of database management systems.
  • Creating and grading assignments and quizzes, offering detailed and constructive feedback to reinforce learning outcomes.
  • Facilitating exercises on real-world database problems, such as cost estimation, query tuning, and schema normalization.
  • Encouraging collaborative learning by fostering a classroom environment where students felt comfortable exploring advanced topics and asking questions.

This role enhanced my ability to explain complex technical concepts effectively, mentor diverse learners, and contribute to studentsâ€™ success in a foundational area of computer science.

Cornell Hyperloop Project Team UI Team Member

As a key member of the Cornell Hyperloop Project Team's User Interface (UI) team, I helped design and develop an advanced Graphical User Interface (GUI) to support the real-time monitoring, visualization, and control of our Hyperloop transoprtation pod. Using Python and the PyQt library, I helped develop a user-friendly interface that created a seamless communication link between the onboard computer and the external GUI.

The GUI was designed to display critical sensor readings, subsystem statuses, and control parameters in real-time. By integrating data collected via Arduino from multiple onboard sensors and subsystems, I ensured the interface provided accurate and timely information. This allowed the team to monitor pod performance metrics such as speed, temperature, pressure, and subsystem integrity, enabling efficient decision-making during testing and operations.

Developing this system required extensive collaboration across teams, including the sensor, mechanical, and software control groups. I worked closely with the sensor team to ensure seamless data integration, with the control team to refine real-time communication protocols, and with the mechanical team to align on subsystem designs and their interaction with the GUI. This interdisciplinary approach was critical to ensuring the system's accuracy, reliability, and operational success.

I was responsible for designing the front-end layout to present complex metrics in a clear, intuitive way while keeping the interface responsive and visually engaging. On the back-end, I integrated real-time data feeds, worked to reduce latency for smoother performance, and ensured the GUI was compatible with various subsystems. I also played a key role in testing and debugging, identifying and fixing issues that could impact data accuracy or system responsiveness.

The GUI improved data accessibility for the engineering team during testing and made troubleshooting faster by clearly highlighting critical system anomalies. This boosted operational efficiency and minimized downtime during test phases. Through this experience, I strengthened my technical skills in Python, PyQt, and hardware-software integration, while also enhancing my collaboration and communication abilities by working closely with multidisciplinary teams to tackle complex engineering challenges.
